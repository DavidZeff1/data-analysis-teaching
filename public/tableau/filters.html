Filters

What Are Filters?

Filters limit the data shown in your visualization. Instead of seeing all 10,000 rows, you can focus on specific regions, time periods, or product categories. Filters are essential for:

  • Focusing analysis - Zoom in on what matters
  • Comparing segments - Look at one region or customer type at a time
  • Creating interactivity - Let viewers choose what they see
  • Improving performance - Less data = faster visualizations

Types of Filters

Dimension Filters

Keep or exclude specific category values

Example: "Show only East and West regions" or "Exclude returns"

Measure Filters

Filter by numeric range or threshold

Example: "Show only orders where Sales > $500" or "Profit between $100 and $1000"

Date Filters

Filter by time period - absolute or relative

Example: "Last 2 years" or "January 2017 to December 2017"

Context Filters

Special filters that apply first - used for "Top N within..."

Example: "Top 10 products within the East region only"

Filter Order of Operations

Tableau applies filters in a specific order. This matters for "Top N" calculations!

  1. 1 Extract Filters (when using extracts)
  2. 2 Data Source Filters
  3. 3 Context Filters ‚Üê Use for "Top N within..."
  4. 4 Dimension Filters
  5. 5 Measure Filters
  6. 6 Table Calculation Filters

üí° Context Filters Explained

If you want "Top 10 products in the East region", you need to make Region a Context Filter first. Otherwise, Tableau calculates Top 10 across ALL regions, then filters to East.

To add to context: Right-click the filter ‚Üí "Add to Context"

Key Intuitions

  • Filters on the shelf are hidden - use "Show Filter" for interactive ones
  • "Exclude" is tempting but risky - if data changes, you might exclude the wrong things
  • Context filters for "Top N within" - this trips up even experienced users
  • Date filters have powerful options - relative dates are great for "always current" views
  • Apply to Worksheets for consistency - especially important for dashboards
  • Filter cards let users explore - essential for self-service analytics
