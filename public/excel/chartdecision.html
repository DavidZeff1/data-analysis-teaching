What is a Chart?

A chart is a visual picture of your data. Instead of looking at rows of numbers in a table, a chart shows patterns, trends, and comparisons at a glance. Our brains process images much faster than text‚Äîa well-designed chart can communicate in seconds what might take minutes to understand from raw numbers.

Why Does Chart Choice Matter?

Choosing the wrong chart is like using the wrong tool for a job:

  • Good chart choice ‚Üí Audience immediately understands your message
  • Poor chart choice ‚Üí Confusion, misinterpretation, or hidden insights

Example: Using a pie chart for 15 categories makes it unreadable. A bar chart would show the same data clearly.

One Numeric Variable

BASIC Line Chart

Standard for emphasizing progression and continuity.

BASIC Sparkline

Miniature line charts for tables/dashboards.

BASIC Bar Chart

Few time periods, emphasize discrete totals.

Comparing Two Points in Time

ADVANCED Slope Chart

Uses line slopes between two temporal points to show direction and magnitude of change for many items.

Two Numeric Variables Across Time

UNCOMMON Connected Scatter Plot

Multiple Recordings Per Time Period

BASIC Box Plot

Shows how a distribution of values evolves over specific intervals.

Financial Metrics

ADVANCED Candlestick Chart

Encodes opening, closing, and price ranges; color indicates direction of change.
